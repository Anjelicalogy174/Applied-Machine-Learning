Conclusion

I conducted a comparative analysis of two widely used classification algorithmsDecision Tree and Random Foreston the well-known Iris dataset. The models were evaluated using accuracy, classification reports, confusion matrices, and visualizations.

Accuracy: Both models achieved perfect accuracy (100%) on the test set, showcasing their effectiveness on this dataset.

Classification Reports: Precision, recall, and F1-scores were all 1.00 across all classes for both models, indicating flawless classification.

Confusion Matrices: Both confusion matrices were perfectly diagonal, confirming no misclassifications.

While both models performed identically on this clean dataset, Random Forest generally offers better generalization on complex or noisy datasets, thanks to its ensemble approach. Decision Trees, though effective here, may be more susceptible to overfitting in such scenarios.

This analysis highlights that for simple, well-structured datasets like Iris, both models perform exceptionally well. However, for real-world applications, Random Forest is usually the more reliable choice due to its robustness.
